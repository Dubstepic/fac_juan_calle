Welcome!

Welcome to my little space on The Web. My name is Juan Calle, and I'm a proud candidate to the winter 2019 cohort of Founders and Coders. Please, feel free to explore my site and get to know me a little bit better,

ABOUT ME

I often think about myself as a swiss knife. I have a pretty broad range of interests that force me to keep learning new things. I crave for knowledge ALL the time. My studies gravitate towards creative media in all its variants: from video production to publishing, all the way to web development. I'm also a self-taught person that has learned 3D modelling, audio production, and anything that gets in the way of my interests. More recently, I have become a certified Dive Master and a Thai masseur, which have become my two new favourite hobbies. Some people think that I invest too much time and effort into learning new things. However, I believe that long-life learning is the secret to exploit our true potential.

Why I learned to code?

Back in Spain in 2012, when I was just starting my career in Media, the economic crisis struck my country. I had to pack my stuff and move to London to start from scratch. At the time, my English skills weren't the best, so I decided to start working in hospitality. That industry gave me a lot of good things but didn't really satisfy my creative ambitions. Thus, I decided to learn to code. Nevertheless, the more I learned, the more I realised that I knew nothing, so I decided to enrol in a Bachelor's in Web Development.

Once finished, and without commercial experience, finding a job proved to be a difficult task. I could have applied for internships, but I didn't have the financial means to afford to live in London without any income, so I was forced to go back to hospitality. Although my career in this field has thrived, I have always dreamed of becoming a full-stack software engineer, and I have tried my best to keep my skills sharp.

Where I want to go?

Firstly, I'd like to break the vicious circle that keeps me coming back to the Hospitality industry. I believe that I have a valuable skillset that can really help to satisfy the needs of many companies. Ideally, I would like to see my career evolving towards a full-stack software engineer. I'm up for any challenge that can keep me motivated, I just need that one first opportunity to step into the tech industry.

WHY F&C

In an era where coding bootcamps have become a large business, mainly focused on profit instead of teaching excellence; I fell in love with the idea of peer-led learning pioneered by Founders & Coders.

I love the idea of giving back and share the knowledge I've gained so far, and the one that is still to come. I wish that one day, I will be able to help others to achieve their dreams in the same way that others have helped me along the way.

WHAT HAVE I LEARNED?

Although I had some previous experience with coding when I started working on my prerequisites, I found my knowledge a bit outdated. Thanks to my application to Founders and Coders, I was able to learn all the new features included in ES 6. I also learned CSS Flexbox and Grid and discovered how much more straightforward the whole process has become. Codewars gave me the ultimate push to realise that I want to code for a living. Even though it was rather hard to rank up, every challenge push my boundaries and allowed me to gain a deeper understanding of algorithms and general Computer Science concepts.

All in all, it's been an intense but fruitful ride, and I hope to be able to put all my new skills to work in meaningful projects with the help of Founders and Coders.

After many years, I decided to quit my thriving career in hospitality and find myself again. I decided to go travelling around Asia and Australia for six months, and here are some neat pics that I took during my time there.

Daintree Rainforest - Australia

The World Heritage-listed is so beautiful that its ancient ferns, emerald green vines and lush canopy provided inspiration for the movie Avatar.

City of Ghosts - Vietnam

Located in An Bang, a quiet village near Thuan An Beach. This local cemetery, with its brilliantly colored structures built on white sand, stretches along the side of the road for 3 kilometers.

Mua Cave - Vietnam

The way leading to Mua Cave is a 500-step zigzag stone staircase. At the end of the staircase is an altar of Quan Am, the Goddess of Mercy, presiding some rather breathtaking views.

Daintree Forest - Australia

At around 1,200 square kilometres, the Daintree is a part of the largest continuous area of tropical rainforest on the Australian continent.

Starfish Beach - Vietnam

On the northern coast of Phu Quoc, a gorgeous beach, totally devoid of other people. Make sure to step cautiously into the water to avoid stepping on the hundreds of starfish strewn about the floor.
